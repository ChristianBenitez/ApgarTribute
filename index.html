Dr. Virginia Apgar

The Creator of the Apgar Score.

The Apgar score is the standardized method of evaluating newborns health minutes after birth

1909: Apgars was born in Westfield New Jersey into a family of 5 with two older siblings

1929: Graduated from Mount Holyoke College with a degree in Zoology and a minor in physiology and Chemistry

1933: Graduated 4th in her class at Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons

1937: Anesthesiology residencies at University of Wisconsin and Bellevue Hospital in NYC

1938: First woman to direct a division as Clinical Director of Division of Anesthesiology at Columbia-Presbyterian Hospital

1949: First full professor of Anesthesiology at Columbia P&S; First woman to hold full profesorship.

1950-52: Devised and tested scoring method to asses newborn viability immediately after birth known as Apgar Score

1953: Published first article on the Apgar Score

1955-58: Correlated score with effects of labor, delivery, and maternal anesthesia on newborns; developed new methods for measuring blood gases, blood levels of anesthesia, and blood pH in newborns

1958-59: Earned Master of Public Health degree at Johns Hopkins

1959: Left P&S and Presbeterian Hospital to join executive medical staff at National Foundation-March of Dimes as Chief of new Division of Congenital Malformations

1964: Recieves honorary D.Med.Sci. from Woman's Medical College of Pennsylvania

1965: Recieves honorary Doctorate from Mount Holyoke College

1965-73: Lecturer and clinical professor of pediatrics (teratology) at Cornell University College of Medicine. First to hold a faculty post in that subspecialty of pediatrics.

1966: Recieved Distinguished Service Award from American Society of Anesthesiologists; recieved Elizabeth Blackwell Medal from American Medical Women's Association

1967: Recieved hororary doctorate from New Jersey College of Medicine and Dentistry

1967-68: Director of Basic Medical Research at National Foundation

1971: Appointed Vice President for Medical Research at National Foundation

1972: Published "Is My Baby All Right?" written with Joan Beck

1973: Received Alumni Gold Medal for Distinguished Achievement from P & S Alumni Association; elected Woman of the Year in Science by Ladies Home Journal; received Ralph Waters Award from American Society of Anesthesiologists

1974: Died August 7th, at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center, of liver failure

1994: Honored on commemorative U.S. postage stamp

1995: Inducted into National Women's Hall of Fame
